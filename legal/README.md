# Kilter — Legal & Commercial Document Drafts

These are **lawyer-ready starter drafts**. They are not legal advice and
must be reviewed and customised by a qualified Ghanaian lawyer before
execution. They are designed so a Ghana lawyer can mark them up rather
than write from scratch — which should cut their billing time
considerably.

## Documents

| # | Document | Purpose | Stage |
|---|---|---|---|
| 01 | [Memorandum & Articles](01_memorandum_and_articles.md) | Ghana Ltd incorporation — name, registered office, objects clause, share capital, directors, custom articles. | Pre-incorporation |
| 02 | [Master Services Agreement (MSA)](02_msa.md) | Production licensing terms for paying customer banks. | Post-pilot conversion |
| 03 | [Pilot Agreement](03_pilot_agreement.md) | Free 60–90 day evaluation with conversion incentives. | First customer touch |
| 04 | [Data Processing Addendum (DPA)](04_dpa.md) | Controller/Processor terms. Incorporated by reference into 02 and 03. | Both pilot and production |
| 05 | [Privacy Policy](05_privacy_policy.md) | Public-facing, for the marketing site and demo signups. | Pre-launch |
| 06 | [Terms of Service](06_terms_of_service.md) | Public-facing, for the marketing site and free demos. | Pre-launch |

## Suggested execution order

1. **Incorporate first.** A Ghana lawyer can take document 01 and file
   the company in 5–10 business days. Estimated all-in cost
   GHS 7,000–18,000 (~USD 500–1,200) including the lawyer's fees, ORC
   filing fees, stamp duty, and the first year of company secretary
   service. Without a Ltd, you cannot sign an MSA in the company's
   name.
2. **Stand up privacy policy + ToS** on the marketing site (documents
   05 and 06) before any public outreach or demo bookings. Both should
   be URL-linked from the site footer and from the demo-request form.
3. **Lock down the Pilot Agreement and DPA** (documents 03 and 04)
   before the first prospect conversation goes anywhere near a real
   bank's procurement team. The pilot doc is the one that gets signed
   first and most often, so it's the most important to get right.
4. **MSA last.** Document 02 only matters after a successful pilot, so
   you have 60–90 days of pilot runway to refine it. Use that time.

## What's intentionally left blank

Throughout the documents, square-bracketed placeholders mark fields the
lawyer or you must fill in:

- **[ADDRESS]**, **[NAME]**, **[DATE]** — fact-specific.
- **[BANK / CUSTOMER LEGAL NAME]** — counterparty-specific.
- **[N]**, **[X%]**, **[USD ___]** — commercial terms negotiated per
  deal.
- **[REGION]**, **[SEAT]**, **[INSTITUTION]** — jurisdiction-specific
  (depends on the customer's preference).

## Things you (or your lawyer) should validate

- **Trademark** — Confirm "Kilter" is clear in the financial-software
  sector before any expensive brand commitment. Earlier session
  identified Kilter (Blackbaud-acquired engagement app) as the main
  moderate–high concern. Budget USD 200–500 for a formal sector-scoped
  search.
- **Capital thresholds** — If foreign shareholders are involved, GIPC
  Act minimum capital requirements apply (currently USD 200k for
  joint-venture services companies; USD 500k for wholly foreign-owned).
- **Sub-processor list (Annex B of the DPA)** — Update before the first
  pilot signature with the actual SMTP / hosting providers you'll use,
  and remove rows for services you don't use.
- **Liability cap and indemnification limits** — Banks will negotiate
  these. Lawyer should set guardrails (your floor) before negotiations
  start.
- **Arbitration seat** — Ghana works for African pilots. For European
  banks expect London (LCIA) or Paris (ICC). Decide what your floor is.
- **Bank-specific regulatory addenda** — Some regulators (BoG, FCA,
  ECB) require specific outsourcing-control language that must be
  added per-bank.

## Things deliberately NOT in this folder (yet)

- **Employment contracts** — needed only when you hire your first
  person. Different doc, different lawyer specialty.
- **IP assignment from founder to company** — needed at incorporation.
  Lawyer typically handles this with a one-page assignment alongside
  the M&A.
- **NDA** — most banks insist on their own NDA for first contact.
  Reactive, not proactive.
- **Investment / SAFE / shareholder agreements** — only relevant when
  raising capital. Premature now.
- **Reseller / partner agreement** — wait for the first partner
  conversation; the structure depends on what they want.

## Open question for you to brief the lawyer on

Before the lawyer engagement, decide:

1. **Founder structure** — solo, or co-founder split? (Affects share
   issuance and vesting.)
2. **Foreign holding company?** — Ghana OpCo + Delaware/UK HoldCo costs
   ~USD 5k more but is the standard pattern for fintechs that plan to
   raise abroad. Skip if you're bootstrapping or only raising in Ghana.
3. **Trademark filing path** — Ghana only (~USD 300, narrow protection)
   versus WIPO Madrid covering Ghana + UK + EU + selected African
   markets (~USD 1,500–3,000, much broader).

Bring written answers to these to the first lawyer meeting — it speeds
everything up.
