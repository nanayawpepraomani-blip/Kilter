The objects for which the Company is established are:

(d) To enter into licensing, reseller, distribution, partnership, and
    sub-contract arrangements with banks, financial institutions, software
    vendors, system integrators, and other counterparties in Ghana and
    abroad.

(e) To acquire, hold, register, license, sub-license, defend, and otherwise
    deal in intellectual property rights — including copyrights,
    trademarks, patents, trade secrets, and database rights — relating to
    the Company's products and services.

(f) To do all such other things as are incidental or conducive to the
    attainment of the foregoing objects.

> Lawyer note: Ghana's Companies Act 2019 permits unlimited objects (s.18),
> but stating a focused objects clause helps with bank-account opening,
> investor due diligence, and tax classification. Avoid generic
> "all-business" language — banks dislike it.

## 2. Articles of Association

The regulations contained in the **First Schedule (Table A)** of the
Companies Act, 2019 (Act 992) shall apply to the Company **except** to the
extent modified by the following provisions:

### 2.1 Share Transfers — Pre-Emption
No member shall transfer any share to a person who is not already a member
of the Company without first offering such share to the existing members in
proportion to their existing holdings, on terms no less favourable than
those proposed to the third party. The board may decline to register any
transfer that does not comply with this article.

### 2.2 Drag-Along (optional — discuss with lawyer)
If members holding [75%] or more of the issued shares accept a bona fide
arms-length offer for 100% of the issued shares, the remaining members
shall be required to sell their shares on the same terms.

### 2.3 Tag-Along (optional — discuss with lawyer)
If a member proposes to sell shares constituting more than [50%] of the
issued shares to a third party, all other members shall be entitled to
participate in the sale on the same terms, pro rata to their holdings.

### 2.4 Reserved Matters Requiring Shareholder Approval
The following matters shall require the prior written approval of members
holding at least [75%] of the issued shares:
- Issue of new shares or convertible instruments.
- Alteration of the rights attaching to any class of shares.
- Sale, transfer, or licence of all or substantially all of the Company's
  intellectual property.
- Incurring debt in excess of GHS [500,000] in aggregate.
- Sale or merger of the Company.
- Voluntary winding-up.

### 2.5 Board Meetings
The board shall meet at least quarterly. A quorum shall be two (2)
directors. Meetings may be held by telephone or video conference.

### 2.6 Indemnity
Subject to the Companies Act, every director, officer, and auditor shall be
indemnified out of the assets of the Company against any liability incurred
in the proper execution of their duties, save in respect of fraud or wilful
misconduct.

## 4. Open Questions for the Lawyer

1. Should the Company be a wholly Ghanaian-owned company, or do you
   anticipate foreign investors? This affects minimum stated capital under
   the GIPC Act.
2. Do you want a holding/operating split (e.g., a Ghana Ltd OpCo and a
   Delaware/UK HoldCo for future investor optics)? Cost: ~$5k more, but
   common for fintech raising abroad.
3. Should founder shares be subject to vesting (typically 4 years, 1-year
   cliff) to protect future co-founders or investors?
4. Will the Company hold the IP, or will the founder license it in via an
   IP-assignment-on-incorporation? Recommended: full assignment to the
   Company at incorporation in exchange for shares.
