1. What Kilter does

Kilter is a nostro-and-GL reconciliation service for correspondent-banking treasuries. For each registered cash account it pairs the correspondent bank's SWIFT statement (MT940/950 or ISO 20022 camt.053/camt.054) against the core-banking GL's posting journal, proposes matches, and carries unresolved breaks forward as a rolling ledger until they clear or are written off.

The system replaces manual reconciliation over Excel workbooks with an auditable web application that:

  • Ingests SWIFT and Flexcube statements automatically on a schedule.
  • Pairs transactions across five tiers of match strength, including many-to-one splits and FX-aware tolerances.
  • Keeps an indelible audit trail — every decision carries who/when/why.
  • Produces the daily breaks workbook and month-end sign-off certificates on demand.
  • Alerts ops when breaks age past their SLA thresholds, via Teams webhook or SMTP email.

2. Who uses it — the four roles

RoleWhat they doPrimary screens
Admin Configures the system — users, registries (cash accounts, currencies, banks, FX rates), tolerance rules, scheduler, SLA channels. Signs certificates. Cash accounts · Scheduler · Users · Match patterns
Ops Drives the daily reconciliation — scans intake, reviews the match queue, clears open items, posts notes, prepares certificates. Dashboard · Sessions · Review · Open items
Internal Control Reviews certificates between preparer and approver (the "checker" in maker-checker-approver). Sanity-checks tolerance exceptions and write-offs. Certificates · Activity log · Open items
Audit Reads the activity log, drills into the history of any break, exports evidence for regulatory review. Activity log · Break history

All roles sign in via Microsoft Authenticator (TOTP). No passwords — your one-time enrollment produces a TOTP secret bound to your account; every subsequent login is a 6-digit code.

3. Key concepts

Session

One session is a single pairing of one SWIFT statement with one Flexcube journal for one registered cash account on one day. A session has an opening balance, a closing balance, a list of SWIFT rows, a list of Flex rows, and the engine's proposed matches between them.

Assignment

The engine's proposed pair between one SWIFT row and one Flex row, tagged with a tier that expresses how confident the match is. Analysts confirm, reject, or skip. Once confirmed, it stays confirmed — no silent edits.

Open item

A SWIFT or Flex row that finished its session without a counterpart. Open items are keyed by account, not session — they outlive session boundaries and can be cleared later by a matching row arriving in a future session (carry-forward), hand-matched by an analyst, or written off with a reason.

Functional group

The team that owns a given break — TREASURY, PSC TROPS, TRADE SWITCHES, CASH MGT, GRA, TPU, CSD FEES. Auto-assigned by rules (user ID, ref prefix, narration keywords); editable per item. Drives the per-team tabs in the daily breaks report.

Certificate

The month-end sign-off artefact. Generated from the live ledger in draft; transitions through prepared (maker) → reviewed (checker) → signed (approver). Signing freezes the figures into an immutable JSON snapshot — later ledger changes cannot rewrite history.

4. A day in the life

Scheduled automation takes Kilter from "a tool" to "a service". A typical business day runs like this:

Time (UTC)What happensWho sees it
05:45Flexcube pull — scheduler runs extract_flex_xlsx.py against Oracle for every registered account, drops xlsx files into messages/flexcube/.Nobody; silent.
06:00–09:00SWIFT files arrive — correspondents' MT940/950 (.out) and ISO 20022 camt.053 / camt.054 (.xml or .txt) drop into messages/swift/ via your existing SFTP pipeline. Some RTGS feeds emit .txt on disk; others send .xml. The scanner auto-detects either.Nobody; silent.
every 15 minIntake scan — scheduler pairs new files to registered accounts, runs the match engine, seeds open items.Dashboard counters update.
07:00Daily breaks report — ready to download at Dashboard → Daily breaks report.Ops inbox / Teams.
07:30SLA alerts fan out — any open item past its threshold triggers the configured Teams / email channels.Functional team inboxes.
08:00 onwardOps walks into Dashboard, clears the review queue, posts notes, writes off stale items.Ops workstation.
last week of monthOps generates month-end certificates at Certificates. Maker prepares → Checker reviews → Approver signs.Certificate desk.
23:00Auto-close — sessions open >12h with no pending rows are closed automatically, seeding their one-sided items into the rolling ledger.Scheduler history.
The pitch line. Corona's reconciliation is session-bound and requires a human to trigger every step. Kilter's ledger outlives sessions and its scheduler runs unattended — once provisioned, the ops team walks in to a reconciled ledger and a ready-to-send report.

5. How it fits beside Corona

Kilter is not a Corona plugin — it's a parallel service that reads the same SWIFT feed and the same core-banking data, and produces the daily breaks workbook and month-end certificates your ops team already recognise. The format is the same; the workflow is automated.

During the transition you may run both in parallel for a period: Kilter in production for the accounts you've registered, Corona as a fallback. The two systems don't talk to each other — Kilter's data is self-contained in its SQLite ledger and can be exported wholesale if ever needed.

6. Quick start — your first reconciliation

Assuming the service is installed and you've been enrolled with MFA:

  1. Sign in at /login. Enter your username and the 6-digit Authenticator code.
  2. Register a cash account. Go to Cash accounts → + New cash account. Pick access area, SWIFT account + BIC, Flexcube GL, currency.
  3. Drop files into intake. Place the day's SWIFT statement — .out (MT940/950), .xml or .txt (camt.053/054) — in messages/swift/ and the matching Flexcube {gl}.xlsx in messages/flexcube/.
  4. Scan. On the Dashboard, click Scan now. New sessions appear in Recent sessions.
  5. Review. Click any session. Use J / K to navigate the queue, Space to confirm, X to reject, N to add a note. Unmatched rows become open items when the session is closed.
  6. Close. Click Close session on the review page. Open items flow into Open items for follow-up.
