Set the scene

It's Monday, 08:00.

You're a reconciliation analyst on the PSC desk. Overnight, 30 correspondent-bank statements dropped into the SFTP pipeline. The Flexcube ledger posted hundreds of journal entries.

In the old world you'd spend the next hour trying to piece them together. In Kilter, it's already done.

→ Switch to the app. Open the Dashboard. Continue with the next slide.

Step 1 / 8 · Dashboard

Ops walks in to a reconciled ledger.

SHOW →

  • Stat counters (pending / confirmed / open sessions / accounts)
  • Match activity sparkline — last 14 days
  • Ageing histogram — 0-1d through 30d+
  • By-functional-team breakdown
  • Recent sessions table

What you're selling

Most legacy reconciliation tools show a list of pending work. This dashboard tells a story — where are we vs last week, what's aging, which team owns what. One screen, one glance.

Pro move: press Ctrl+K → "Presenter mode" for the big-screen moment.

Step 2 / 8 · Review queue

Confirm a match in one keystroke.

SHOW →

  • Open a session from Recent sessions
  • Tier banner (green = tier 1 = confident)
  • SWIFT left, Flex right, tier reason on top
  • Space confirm · X reject · J next
  • Rapid-fire: clear five matches in <10 seconds
  • N for a note · ? for keyboard help

The talking beat

"A hundred pending matches — not a hundred clicks. Space. Space. Space. When a weaker-tier match comes up, slow down, check the narration, make the call."

Step 5c / 8 · Cards

Scheme settlements, PCI scope reduced by design.

SHOW →

  • Sidebar → Cards
  • PCI banner: first6 + last4 only, never full PAN; CVV / track / PIN refused
  • Files tab: scheme tiles (Visa, Mastercard, Verve, Cardlink GhIPSS)
  • Matches tab: filter chips (All · Mismatched · Matched · Unmatched)
  • Click Records → on a mismatched group — drawer opens
  • Drawer: per-record breakdown across files, masked PAN cell, status pill

The talking beat

"Cards is where most reconciliation tools either don't go or drag your bank deeper into PCI scope. Kilter does neither. The schema has no column that could hold a full PAN. Loaders mask at the parser seam. CVV and track-data fields are refused on ingest. The N-way match engine joins on scheme reference, classifies each group, and surfaces mismatches first — your operators investigate the highest-leverage cases without scrolling."

Step 6 / 8 · Certificates

Month-end sign-off with segregation of duties.

SHOW →

  • Ctrl+K → "Certificates"

  • Click PDF 🖨 — print-ready page opens
  • Point at the 3 signature boxes: Maker, Checker, Approver
  • Describe the flow: Ops prepares → IC reviews → Admin signs
  • "Signing freezes the figures — immutable from that moment"

The talking beat

"Most legacy reconciliation stacks have no equivalent. This is the regulator's 'show me your sign-off trail' question, answered structurally — not by PDF-ing an email thread."

Step 7 / 8 · Scheduler

The engine room.

SHOW →

  • Ctrl+K → "Scheduler"
  • Green pill = daemon running
  • Five seeded jobs: scan · daily_close · sla_check · report · flex_extract
  • Next run times visible per job
  • Click History on any job — last 20 runs with timings
  • Click Run now on the scan job — fires immediately

The talking beat

"Legacy tools require a person for every pull, every scan, every report. Kilter's calendar runs the day. Ops investigates the exceptions; the machine does the rest."

If asked about formats: the scan job accepts MT940/950 .out, ISO 20022 camt.053/054 .xml, and any BYO CSV / xlsx profile side-by-side — no reconfiguration.
